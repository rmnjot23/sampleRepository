Aurora Borealis

What are they ?

The auroras are a natural phenoma in which an array of lights paint the skies. The colors can vary from red to pink

What causes them?

Due to the earths magnetic field and the incoming rays from the sun we see the colors of the auroras. The magnetic field is strongest at the poles. As the sun's energy reaches the poles certain particles of gases such as oxygen and nitrogen have a shift and began to take color. Diffrent gasses have different colors

Colors, Gases and Distance

  • Pink-^Nitrogen^-Under 100km from ground
  • Green^Oxygen^100-200 km above
  • Blue^Nitrogen^100-200km
  • Red^Oxygen^Above 200km
